It is a famous Roman Catholic pilgrim centre and is visited by thousands of devotees for its shrine of ‘Our Lady of Good Health’ aka Mother Mary. Inspired by the Lourdes in France, Velankanni is also known as the “Lourdes of the East”. It has become one of the vital Marian sanctuaries of the world.

state:- Tamilnadu 


histroy:
The History of Vellore dates back to the 9th century to which the Chola Inscriptions in the Annamalaiyar Temple of Tiruvannamalai stands evidence. Along with the rich cultural influences seen here due to the different kingdoms that ruled the region, Vellore has seen as many battles and deaths too. The Vellore fort which is said to have been built during Channa Bommi Nayaka and Thimma Reddy Nayaka in the 15th century and happens to be one of the best examples of military architecture in South India. Apart from being a wonder in itself, the Fort also holds some other essential structures such as the Jalagandeeshwar Temple, St. John’s Church and The Archeological Survey of India Museum that depict the history in their unique way.

details:-
An ancient city in the northeastern part of Tamil Nadu, Vellore is located on the banks of river Palar Vellore. Nicknamed ‘The Fort City’ due to the marvellous Vellore Fort which sits at the heart of Vellore city, it is an ideal place to glimpse Tamil Nadu's rich cultural heritage through its temples.
Contributing for 37% of the country's total leather export, Vellore is primarily known for two premier institutions, Christain Medical College and Vellore Institute of Technology. Apart from this, the city is known for the Ratnagiri Temple and Roman Catholic Diocese.
